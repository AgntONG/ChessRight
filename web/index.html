The most beautiful move ever played.

For over a century, one move has topped every "greatest ever" list. Marshall placed his queen en prise three different ways — and every capture loses. Legend says spectators showered the board with gold.

01

The threat

After 23…Qg3!!, Black threatens …Qxh2# — mate in one. White must answer the queen immediately.

02

The trap

Three pieces can capture the queen: the h-pawn, the f-pawn, and White's own queen. Yet every capture walks into forced mate or a lost endgame.

03

The point

The knight on d4 is the silent hero. It delivers the smothered mates via …Ne2# and …Nf3+ the moment a pawn steps forward.

Every defence loses

Levitsky resigned without making another move. Here is what the engine sees after each try:

  • 24. hxg3 → Ne2# Smothered mate — the king is choked by its own pieces.
  • 24. fxg3 → Ne2+ Kh1 Rxf1# Back-rank mate on the newly opened f-file.
  • 24. Qe5 → Nf3+ Kh1 Rxh2# A knight fork paves the way for the rook.
  • 24. f4 / f3 → Ne2+ Kh1 Qxh2# The queen simply takes its rightful square.
  • 24. Rf1+ → Rxf1 Qxh2# Even giving up the rook doesn't avert mate on h2.
Under the hood

How ChessRight thinks.

A modern engine is two systems: a search that explores millions of positions, and an evaluation that scores them. ChessRight runs both at the frontier of what's possible.

Alpha-Beta Search

Prunes whole branches of the move tree that can't change the verdict, reaching 30+ ply in the middlegame. Late-move reductions, null-move pruning, and aspiration windows add another order of magnitude.

NNUE Evaluation

An efficiently-updatable neural net scores positions in nanoseconds on the CPU — no GPU required. Trained on billions of self-play positions, it reads piece activity and king safety like a grandmaster.

Syzygy Tablebases

Perfect play from seven pieces down. The 50-move rule, distance-to-zero, and DTZ are all handled — endgames stop being a fight and become a lookup.

UCI Protocol

Drop ChessRight into any GUI — ChessBase, Arena, cutechess, lichess-bot. It speaks the universal uci dialect and works everywhere Stockfish does.

Anatomy of a move

What the eval bar saw.

Why a "blunder" becomes a win

The search must prove that every capture of the queen loses. At shallow depth the horizon effect lets White "win the queen" with no downside. By depth 30 the knight's smothered mates and back-rank threats fall inside the search window — and the eval collapses to −M.
